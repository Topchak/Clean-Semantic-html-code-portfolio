16.15.1 Over/under means betting that is determined by the total number of points (or goals, games etc.) in the final result of an Event. 16.15.2 First half over/under means betting that is determined by the total number of points in the result of the first half of an Event. 16.16.1 Odd/even means betting that is determined by whether the total number of points (or goals, games etc.) in the final result of an event is odd or even. For all odd/even markets zero is counted as even for settlement purposes.

Bets will also be considered valid if an official result is declared by the relevant governing body. Otherwise bets on suspended or abandoned matches will be void, except for those on markets which have been unconditionally determined.

17.1.4  First-Half results are the sum of the First and Second Quarters. Second-Half results are the sum of the Third and Fourth Quarters, including any Overtime that may be played.

17.1.5 Fourth Quarter results, Quarter with the most points and Double Result markets do not include any Overtime that may be played.

17.1.6 If a match is suspended or abandoned then bets placed on unfinished Halves or Quarters will be considered void. If the designated Halves or Quarters are completed then bets will be valid. As will all bets on any market already unconditionally determined.

17.3 Baseball

17.3.1 All full-time markets, including Live Betting, will be settled on the final result NOT including extra innings except for ‘Who score the Xth point (run) and ‘Which Team will win race to X points’ or otherwise stated. In Japanese or Korean baseball a tie can be declared and in this case moneyline bets will be refunded.

17.3.2 If a game does not start on the scheduled start date (or within 12 (twelve) hours of the scheduled start time) then all bets will be void (unless stated otherwise). If a game is postponed or cancelled before its due start time, all bets are deemed No Action.

17.3.3 For baseball bets to be considered valid then the game must go nine innings (or 8.5 (eight and five tenths) innings if the home team is leading). If a game is ‘called’ or suspended and completed on a subsequent day then all bets (except those which have been unconditionally determined) will be considered void. Specifically for MLB, if a game is ‘called’, or suspended, the winner is determined by the score after the last full inning (unless the home team scores to tie, or takes the lead in the bottom half of the inning, in which case the winner is determined by the score at the time the game is called.) Stakes will be refunded if the home team ties the game and it is then suspended. If a game is ‘called’, or suspended, winners and losers for betting (including Totals) are official after at least 5 (five) innings of play unless the home team is leading after 4.5 (four and five tenths) innings. Other markets will be void unless a winning market has already been established.

17.3.4 If a game is suspended or called in extra innings then the score will be determined after the last full inning, unless the home team scores to tie or lead in the bottom half of the inning, in which case the score is determined at the time the game is called.

17.3.5 First 5 (five) innings bets are settled on the result at the end of five innings. If five innings are not completed, for whatever reason, then all bets will be considered void.
